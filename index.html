THE
PROBLEM:

It can be difficult to gauge symptoms and stressors to determine which treatment methods are ideal for the patient. Current stress monitoring practices can be inaccurate and subjective, or the equipment used is generally inaccessible. Other available devices are expensive or do not allow health professionals to view the data remotely.

THE SOLUTION:

StressWatch

The StressWatch is a stress monitoring device that allows for real-time detection of stress levels based on the skin conductance response.
How It Works

During a stress response, a person experiences an increase in body temperature, causing perspiration. Skin conductance, also known as a galvanic skin response or GSR, is a phenomenon when the skin becomes a better conductor of electricity when one is physiologically aroused, meaning there is an emotional response.

Using a GSR sensor, the StressWatch collects data on human resistance, a measure of the opposition to current flow on a person’s skin. Significant drops, or lower values, in human resistance indicate greater stress, as there is more sweat and therefore, more current.
